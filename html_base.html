In this chapter, you have learned how background images can be applied to elements to produce a variety of interesting techniques, such as flexible rounded-corner boxes and pure CSS drop shadows. You have also seen how the onset of new CSS 3 properties like border-radius and box-shadow are beginning to make these effects redundant. You have learned all about opacity and seen how to force PNG support in Internet Explorer along with several methods of image replacement.
